Bio:

Kianté Brantley is an Assistant Professor in the Kempner Institute and School of Engineering and Applied Sciences (SEAS) at Havard University. He completed his Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of Maryland College Park, advised by Dr. Hal Daumé III. After graduating, he completed his postdoctoral studies at Cornel University, working with Thorsten Joachims. His research focuses on developing machine learning models that can interact and make automated decisions in the real world with minimal supervision. He has received several awards with his colleagues, including spotlight talks at ICLR 2023 and ICLR 2019. He is a recipient of the NSF LSAMP BD Fellowship, ACM SIGHPC Computational and Data Science Fellowship, Microsoft Dissertation Research Grant, Ann G. Wylie Dissertation Fellowship, and NSF CIFellow Postdoctoral Fellowship.In his spare time, he enjoys playing sports; his favorite sport at the moment is powerlifting.
